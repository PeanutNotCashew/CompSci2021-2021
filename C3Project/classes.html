Courses

Higher Level Courses

History of the Americas

Students learn world history, as well as the history of the Americas. They study various types of history, such as political, economic, and cultural history.

Teachers

Robert Caccamo | Cris Johnson | Steven Rabinowitz | Mark Wolov

Language A

Students study texts of multiple cultures, formats, and time periods. They learn how to analyse literature in their main language.

Teachers

Michelle Gallo | Maria Klidonas | Juanita Kumar | Jamie Meisler | Chris Potter

Visual Art

Students learn about the cultural contexts around different artworks, different techniques for making art, and explore different ways of communicating meaning through art.

Teachers

Terry Clarkson-Farrell | Lucas Sheridan | Stephanie Tramontozzi

Computer Science

Students learn programming skills as well as other computer science related subjects, such as webscience or databases.

Teachers

Shantanu Saha

Standard Level Courses

Biology

Students study topics from the structure of a cell to how an ecosystem functions. This course focuses on a practical approach to the sciences.

Teachers

Dr. Kevin Helfenbein| Milena Mihalache | Sonia Silva

Chemistry

Students study the chemical principles that establish the groundwork for other courses, such as medicine and environmental science. This course focuses on a practical approach to the sciences.

Teachers

Dr. Kevin Helfenbein | Milena Mihalache | Brian Yanek

Mathematics: Applications and Interpretation

Students develop mathematical knowledge and logical thinking. This mathematics course focuses on modelling, statistics, and practical application.

Teachers

Ana Harris | Jim Heine | Michael Mehan | Hweeyong Mun | Jim Napolitano | Lambrini Tampouratzi

Mathematics: Analysis and Approaches

Students develop mathematical knowledge and logical thinking. This mathematics course focuses on calculus and theoretics.

Teachers

Ana Harris | Jim Heine | Michael Mehan | Hweeyong Mun | Jim Napolitano | Lambrini Tampouratzi

Language B

Students learn to communicate in a foreign language, both through writing and orally. BSGE offers classes in either French or Spanish.

Teachers

Lucia Africano | Freddy Giraldo | Liberata Kadende

Other Courses

Theory of Knowledge

Theory of Knowledge aims to make students aware of the interpretive nature of knowledge, such as biases. It allows students to reevaluate their own biases and assumptions.

Teachers

Maria Klidonas | Jaime Meisler | Chris Potter

Physical Education

Students develop motor skills and have opportunities to play in team sports. Students generally have physical education classes outside at a nearby park.

Teachers

Patrick McDonald | Jacques Powell
